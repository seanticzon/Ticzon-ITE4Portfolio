Term Reflection

One of the more Interesting topics we learned about was Artificial Intelligence and its functions during our first term. Artificial Intelligence is involved with our daily lives and lets machines perform tasks that have human capabilities. They can solve difficult problems or can do reasoning, learn from experience through training, and lend a hand in creative functions. For me, artificial intelligence has greatly helped coding, especially debugging. Getting errors from long blocks of code can be frustrating and time-consuming as well, but with AI one can debug much faster by identifying the problems that can save one's time and efforts.

AI has many important branches, one of which is Machine Learning (ML). It is concerned with giving the computer the ability to perform tasks without explicit programming. Another branch is Deep Learning, which is based on neural networks in processing data and finding patterns.

We covered the various forms of Machine Learning in our class:

  • • Supervised Learning: Uses labeled training data for prediction, like email filtering ("spam"/"ham") or sentiment analysis.
  • • Unsupervised Learning: Works with unlabeled data by finding patterns or clustering in data.
  • • Reinforcement Learning: Improves through rewards and penalties based on actions taken.

Mostly in Machine Learning it has a few steps it follows. It reads and analyzes the pattern in the features of the data, maps that into labels, and can look at the new data and recognize the pattern. The patterns are stored in the model, allowing it to make predictions based on learning.

Then, we learned NLP Text Preprocessing. NLP or Natural Language Processing is part of Artificial Intelligence mainly used for the understanding and processing of human language. "Text" refers to any message or information that is written or printed- such as books, comments, emails, or news articles.

Preprocessing includes multiple important phases:

  • • Lowercasing: Converting all characters to lower cases
  • • Special Character Removal: Removing punctuation marks, symbols, and numbers
  • • Stopword Removal: Eliminating common words with minor meaning
  • • Tokenization: Breaking text into smaller portions (tokens)

Key Terms in NLP Text Representation:

  • • Corpus: Collection of text data or records
  • • Vocabulary: Unique words within the corpus
  • • Document: Individual text record within the corpus
  • • Word: Single unit found in the vocabulary

Text Representation Methods:

  • • One-Hot Encoding: Creates unique vectors with one "1" element
  • • Bag of Words (BoW): Calculates word occurrences
  • • Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency: Weights words based on occurrence patterns
  • • Word Embeddings: Maps words to dense vectors capturing semantic relationships

Learning about Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing has really been a step up for me in understanding how machines interpret human language. Concepts such as text preprocessing, text representation, and NLP techniques have made it possible for me to not only gain theoretical knowledge but also actual skills on the transformation of text into a machine-readable format. The coding and debugging skills are already enhanced mainly with regard to optimizing time-consuming tasks.

This experience enables me to keep developing and learn about AI, to develop applications for easier and more efficient use in everyday work. In the short term, It gave me a drive to learn deeper into knowledge about machine learning models and work hands-on experience through NLP projects. Setting such goals motivates me to keep expanding myself in the aspects of how AI can impact fields from language processing to creative problem-solving.
