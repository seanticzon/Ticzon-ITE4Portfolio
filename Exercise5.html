Personal Reflection

For the BERT Question and Answering models from Hugging Face, the experience here was fun to test each of the pre-trained models. The pre-trained models that I used are:

  • • Small model (anas-awadalla/bert-small-finetuned-squad)
  • • Base model (deepset/bert-base-cased-squad2)
  • • Large model (phiyodr/bert-large-finetuned-squad2)
  • • Albert (TunahanGokcimen/Question-Answering-Albert-base)
  • • RoBERTa (consciousAI/question-answering-roberta-base-s)
  • • DistilBERT (distilbert/distilbert-base-cased-distilled-squad)

Each model performs well, but the RoBERTa QnA pre-trained model surprised me. It showed good results in Question and Answering, scoring 9/10 on 10 questions.

For this activity, it was also exciting to see what the answers of each pre-trained model were, and it was a good experience for me to learn the differences among the models used here and their capabilities.

Mr. Ticzon's paper provides a comprehensive overview of using BERT-based models for question answering tasks. The paper effectively outlines the methodology, including data preparation, model selection, and evaluation. The paper's strengths lie in its clear explanation of the concepts and the practical implementation using the Hugging Face library. The step-by-step guide and code snippets make it easier for readers to replicate the experiments. However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved: Evaluation Metrics: While the paper mentions the importance of evaluating models, it lacks a detailed discussion of specific metrics used to assess the performance of the models. Metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score could be used to quantify the models' performance. Comparison of Models: A more in-depth comparison of the performance of different BERT variants (BERT, RoBERTa, DistilBERT) would be beneficial. Analyzing the trade-offs between model size, performance, and computational cost could provide valuable insights. Data Quality and Quantity: Discussing the impact of data quality and quantity on the performance of the models could add value to the paper. High-quality and diverse datasets are crucial for training effective language models. Model Limitations and Future Directions: Acknowledging the limitations of BERT-based models, such as their reliance on large amounts of data and computational resources, would be helpful. Additionally, exploring potential future directions, such as fine-tuning models on specific domains or incorporating knowledge graphs, could be discussed. Overall, Mr. Ticzon's paper provides a solid foundation for understanding and implementing BERT-based question answering systems. By addressing the suggested improvements, the paper can be further enhanced to provide a more comprehensive and insightful analysis.
