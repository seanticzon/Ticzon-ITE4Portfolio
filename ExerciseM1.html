Personal Reflection

For the Implementing of NLP application models using Spacy, our task was to add 30 or more data for each classification task. The classification tasks are the following:

  • • Email Classification: Identifying whether the email is SPAM or HAM.
  • • Named Entity Recognition: It refers to the process of extracting and categorizing entities in a text according to pre-defined categories such as names of people, organizations, locations, dates, etc.
  • • Part of Speech Tagging: Assigns each word of the sentence a grammatical category: nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and so on.
  • • Sentiment Analysis: It is a 3-point scale classifying text as either positive, negative, or neutral.
  • • Text Summarization: A technique that involves condensing large volumes of text into shorter, coherent summaries while preserving the key information and meaning.

I added manual training data here, and they are labeled according to the classification task. For the Email Classification, I added the body of the legitimate email and its label "HAM" and the body of the spam email and labeled it as "SPAM".

For the Named Entity Recognition or NER, I added a sentence or the headlines of news articles and carefully counted the indexes of the characters, including whitespaces, for naming entities. Examples of labels are "ORG" for organizations and "PERSON" for persons.

In the Part of Speech Tagging, it is also similar to NER, but we are going to tag each word or text in the sentence according to their grammatical category. Example labels are "NOUN", "VERB", "PUNCT", "ADJ", etc.

For the Sentiment Analysis, it is also similar to SPAM and HAM, adding the text for online product reviews. Example labels for each are "POSITIVE", "NEGATIVE", and "NEUTRAL".

Lastly, for the text summarization, we are going to input a long text that the text summarization model will identify and extract the relevant information, decreasing the text size for a summary that will capture the key points.
