Exercise Reflection

The things that I learned about this activity is that the text preprocessing techniques performed, such as Lowercasing, Special Character Removal, Stopword Removal, and Tokenization, will impact the quality of the text analysis in the news articles.

Key Text Preprocessing Techniques:

  • • Lowercasing: it made the text uniform and reduced the number of unique terms in the textual data.
  • • Special Character Removal: removing some non-alphanumeric characters made the text cleaner and reduced the noise within the text.
  • • Stopword Removal: this also reduced the terms in the textual data and decreased the complexity of the dataset.
  • • Tokenization: helped in text division by turning sentences or words into individual tokens, making the structure ready and available for different text classifications or analyses.

This resulted in organized text data that will improve fake news detection, leading to more precise text classification. I also noticed that some text preprocessing steps might affect text classification in sentiment analysis; some special characters, such as punctuation marks, might also impact the overall performance of sentiment analysis classification.

Peer Review

First Comment: I feel, Mr. Ticzon can do to improve this project is the examination and evaluation of some specific preprocessing techniques. While their work provides very nice coverage of why preprocessing is important, deeper analysis on how different methods impact various NLP tasks is needed. For example, granular comparison of tokenization, stop word removal, and stemming, and how these interacted with the performance of machine learning models, would have added a lot of value to their findings. This could be improved on by relating such preprocessing techniques to exact challenges in the dataset they are working on about the language itself, noise, or variability of text structure. It would also benefit from including a section on the limitations of preprocessing techniques in and of themselves, and how these might affect the outcomes of subsequent NLP tasks. In this regard, based on my experience, some methods of preprocessing do improve the quality of data significantly, but if applied inappropriate, they can introduce bias or scrub important contextual information. Discussion of these possible trade-offs at appropriate places will make their work paint a clearer picture on how preprocessing impacts data analytics. Nevertheless, my work supports only the general ideas brought forth by my colleague, emphasizing the high importance of preprocessing in NLP and that there are a lot of useful ideas concerning news article analysis. However, I will argue that closer and deeper looks at certain techniques of preprocessing, which I did in my work, give more relevance and applicability to his findings. Their work may, therefore, ring more effectively towards the niche of NLP and data analytics if only inculcation of more profound analysis and handling of the specific challenges in their dataset have been taken care of.
