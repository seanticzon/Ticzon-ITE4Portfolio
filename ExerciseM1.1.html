While looking through your work, I found some concerns that should be addressed in order to enhance the performance of natural language processing models. One of the major problems is “overfitted.” This arises when a model fits the training data to the extent of remembering the answers rather than learning the pattern. Consequently, although it performs well during training, and testing and yields a 100% accuracy rate, it fails when confronted with new, actual values it has never learned about. This is like a student who prepares cram notes to use when answering a particular instructor’s questions but cannot use the same to solve other questions or even practical real-life problems. Another problem is the nature of the training data, where most of the data taken is of poor quality. There is the possibility of using the obtained datasets in this study not reflecting the vastness and diversification of the textual data in the real world. For example, language can be raw and messy, full of slang, typos, and a plethora of expression varieties, but if the training dataset is too neat or fairly standardized, the model may be poor in handling such variations. This may cause a situation whereby the model works well at the research level but does not produce the right results when used in real-world assessment. Moreover, one thing that works for almost all models is that the more data available the better the model will be but here too, it is important to state the quality of the data. Just getting more data without concern for relevance and quality will eventually decrease the effectiveness. If testing is not done well, it may create a good impression that the model is performing better as it is not the real case. In view of these problems, you need to use richer and more realistic samples as your training material that could approximate real-life language use. This could involve using data from social media posts, customer feedback, and other informal texts. It is also necessary to apply stricter techniques of validation to make sure that the model can apply knowledge of learning to new conditions. It will also be important to routinely expose the model to more difficult and diverse samples in order to determine the model’s reliability in practical applications. That way, there are better chances of having a more dependable and efficient natural language processing model that will work best in real-world practice.
